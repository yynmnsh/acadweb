    Making AI Accessible

    I believe that artificial intelligence and machine learning should not be mysterious black boxes. My teaching approach focuses on demystifying complex algorithms and helping students understand not just how AI works, but why it works and when it might fail.

    Current Courses (Spring 2025)

    CS 4780: Introduction to Machine Learning

    Undergraduate | Spring 2025 | MWF 10:00-10:50 AM

    A comprehensive introduction to machine learning covering supervised and unsupervised learning, neural networks, and practical applications. Students will implement algorithms from scratch and work on real-world datasets.

    Topics Covered:

    • Linear regression and classification
    • Decision trees and ensemble methods
    • Neural networks and deep learning
    • Clustering and dimensionality reduction
    • Model evaluation and validation
    Syllabus Course Materials Schedule Assignments

    CS 6780: Ethics in Artificial Intelligence

    Graduate | Spring 2025 | TR 2:30-3:45 PM

    An advanced seminar exploring the ethical implications of AI systems. Students will read cutting-edge research papers, engage in debates, and develop frameworks for ethical AI development.

    Key Areas:

    • Algorithmic bias and fairness
    • Privacy and surveillance
    • Autonomous systems and responsibility
    • AI governance and policy
    • Future of work and AI
    Reading List Discussion Forum Final Projects

    CS 7990: Research Seminar in AI Safety

    Graduate | Spring 2025 | F 1:00-3:00 PM

    A research-focused seminar where graduate students present their work and discuss current challenges in AI safety. Guest speakers from industry and academia share their perspectives on building safe and beneficial AI systems.
